# sample_photos.png

**File:** `results/sample_photos.png`
**Step:** 2 — look at the data

**What it shows:** A grid of example photos from the HAM10000 dataset. Seven rows, one per lesion type, with four sample images in each row. The rows are harmless mole (nv), melanoma (mel), benign keratosis (bkl), basal cell carcinoma (bcc), pre-cancer / actinic keratosis (akiec), vascular lesion (vasc), and dermatofibroma (df).

**How to read it:** Each row is labelled on the left with its lesion type. These are dermatoscopic close-ups, taken with a magnifying lens pressed against the skin, so they are evenly lit, in focus, and centred.

**Takeaway:** This is the raw data in its clean, ideal form, and it sets the "easy" starting condition for the study. Two things are worth noticing: several types look similar to the eye (melanoma vs a harmless mole is not obvious), which is why the task is hard; and every photo here is clean, unlike the phone photos real people take, which is exactly what the later steps stress-test.
